Abattoir: a place where animals are killed, inspected, and butchered.

Berkshire: a heritage breed of pig prized for its fat and flavour

Blade steak - butt chop: steak cut from the upper shoulder or butt that included part of shoulder blade, also known as butt chop

Butt: (pork) upper shoulder

Commercial pigs: (Commodity pork) Pigs raised in an industrial farming method, to get them to market weight as quickly and nutritionally as possible

Duroc: breed of pig, common pig used in Canadian large-scale farming

Flock of laying hens: several hens used for egg production

Foragers: (in animals) to search and or dig for food using their snout

Grass fed beef: Beef raised entirely on grass, no grain

Grass fed sheep: Sheep raised entirely on grass, no grain

Hanging weight: weight of an animal after slaughter and evisceration

Holistic: to approach a situation by considering all needs or angles. It can relate to sustainability by considering environmental, social and economic factors

Kobe beef: a highly marbled breed of Japanese beef (see Beef Module for further info)

Live weight: weight of an animal before slaughter

Marbling / intramuscular fat: intramuscular fat in muscle

Sustainable: there are many definitions of sustainability, the United Nations defines it as “meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.”
