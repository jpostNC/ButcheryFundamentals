Aitch bone: (Aitchbone, Aitch-bone) is the back section of the pelvic bone which is connected to the hip bone

Femur: The bone that holds the thigh meat in one place.

Hock: a part of the pig, hock refers to the joint between the Tibia and Trotter

Patella: bone, refers to the kneecap

Reverse grip or pistol grip or dagger grip: a way of holding a knife by clenching the handle so the blade points down towards the floor, holding a blade this way is an effective way of manipulating the tip of the blade around round bones.

Seam: the connection where one muscle ends and another begins. Seam cutting is a style of butchering, where the butcher separates individual muscles by following where they connect with the knife.

Silverskin: Is a thin membrane of connective tissue that exists on some muscles. Silverskin tissue gets tougher with age.

Sirloin Tip Knuckle: Also known as a cushion, it is used for grilling, stewing, grinding. It has a chewier texture. It is also known as tip steak. Inside round
