Pigs are not native to North America. They were introduced to the new world by Europeans.

Pig: a pig under one year of age when slaughtered. Usually between 5 and 5 months old. Market weight 280 lbs / 125kg

Gilt - Female pig that has never had a litter

Sow - name of gilt, after delivering their first litter

Boar - intact male pig used for breeding purposes

Barrow - male pig that has been neutered

Suckling pig - a young pig slaughtered between 2-6 weeks old

Breeds: common breeds raised in Ontario for commodity pork are Yorkshire, Landrace and Duroc.
