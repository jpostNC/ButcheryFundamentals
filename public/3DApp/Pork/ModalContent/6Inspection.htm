Inspection: All pigs are subject to provincial and federal inspection. Inspection provides a guarantee of wholesomeness and that the product is fit for human consumption. After the animal has been inspected and deemed fit for human consumption, the inspector will stamp 4 stamps on the pig carcass which are positioned on primal cuts.

Heritage breeds: Heritage breeds like Tamworth, Berkshire and Mangalitsa fetch a price cost than commodity breeds.

Pigs are not given growth hormones in Canada.

Inspection is required by Canadian law.

Inspection Stamp - stamps are made of edible natural coloring agents: such as alkanet, annatto, carotene, cochineal, green chlorophyll, saffron, and turmeric.
