Pork flavour varies within different breeds and also on what they eat.
In fatty breeds like Berkshire and Red Wattle, the flavour ranges from buttery and creamy to earthy and vegetal.
Lean breeds like Duroc and Tamworth, have clean and mild flavour which is slightly sweet. (Lee, 2014)

In commodity pork, where pigs are raised more for meat rather than fat content, the flavour is mild and neutral, which makes it ideal meat for incorporating additional flavours of spices and marinades.

Internal Cooking Temperature

Tender cuts of pork should be cooked to Medium, 160℉ (71℃). This includes the leg, loin and rack

Tough cuts of pork should be cooked low and slow at 225℉/107℃ so that the connective tissue can break down, to an internal temperature of approximately 190 ℉ (88℃) This includes shoulder, belly, shank and ribs.

Ground pork should be cooked to 160 ℉ (71℃).
(Ontario Pork)
