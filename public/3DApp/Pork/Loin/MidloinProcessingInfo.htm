Brining: a method of curing and flavouring where salt (and sometimes other flavour additives like spices) is mixed with water to create a brine. Meat is submerged and left to cure for a period of time.

Butterfly Chop: a chop that has been sliced to open it like a butterfly, meant to make it thinner for quick cooking, and also makes it appear larger. Can be boneless or bone-in.

Chop: (Noun) a steak cut from the loin sirloin or rib can be with or without bone.

Chop: (verb) in the kitchen: to cut finely; In the butcher shop: to split a bone, usually with a cleaver

Fast fry chop: a thinly cut chop meant to be cooked quickly.

Peameal Bacon: Brined loin of pork that is rolled in cornmeal, often used as breakfast meat in Canada. Peameal was the original meal that is was rolled in- hence the name “Peameal bacon”

Pin bones: also referred to as feather bones on quadrupeds. In fish pin bones are thin bones located in the fillets, closer to the head of the fish.

Tenderloin: The most tender cut on any animal. On 4 legged animals the tenderloin muscle starts at the ribs and runs into the leg. It is connected to the primal loin cut of beef and pork and the primal saddle cut of lamb.

Vertebrae: the backbone or spine of the animal.
