Crown: (Poultry) the top part of the bird once the legs are removed. Contains breasts, whole wings, and backbone.

Dark meat: Meat from the legs and thighs of chicken and turkeys is referred to as dark meat due to its darker colour than the breasts or wings. Dark meat has more fat, flavour and connective tissue than white meat.

Leg: (poultry) the lower part of a bird, contains the thigh and drumstick. Can be purchased with part of the backbone attached. Located at the bottom of the chicken leg. Contains elastin and thicker connective tissue.

Thigh: (poultry) The meatiest part of the leg, it is full of collagen, and carries more flavour than the breast. It is located on the top part of the leg and is attached to the backbone.

White meat: meat from the breasts and wings of chickens and turkeys are referred to as white meat because of its light colour. White meat has less fat, flavour, and connective tissue than dark meat.
