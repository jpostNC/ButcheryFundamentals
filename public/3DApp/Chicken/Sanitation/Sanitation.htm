Bacteria: “are small single-celled organisms. Bacteria are found almost everywhere on Earth and are vital to the planet's ecosystems. Some species can live under extreme conditions of temperature and pressure. The human body is full of bacteria, and in fact is estimated to contain more bacterial cells than human cells. Most bacteria in the body are harmless, and some are even helpful. A relatively small number of species cause disease.”

https://www.genome.gov/genetics-glossary/Bacteria

Cross contamination: It is the physical movement of transferring bacteria from one surface to another.

Salmonella: One of the most common types of food poisoning caused by bacteria. Salmonella lives in the intestinal tract of animals. It is transmitted by eating food contaminated with animal feces. Ways of preventing salmonella:

  1. Wash your hands
  2. Always keep your food preparation surfaces and equipment clean and sanitized
  3. Store food correctly and at the appropriate temperatures
  4. Cook food to the appropriate temperature
