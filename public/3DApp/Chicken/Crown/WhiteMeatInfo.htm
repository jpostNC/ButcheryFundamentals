Crown: (Poultry) the top part of the bird once the legs are removed. Contains breasts, whole wings, and backbone.

Drumette, the drum of the wing: The first part of the wing attached to the breast. It is the meatiest part of the wing and resembles the chicken drumstick, though smaller.

Joint: connections where bones meet. (In the UK “Joint of Beef” refers to a roasted piece of beef)

Keel bone: (poultry) breastbone that runs along the middle of the chicken's cavity. An excellent source for making chicken stock

Supreme: chicken breast removed from the ribcage and keel, the wing drumette is still attached to the breast. The skin can be left on or removed.

Wing Flat: The middle part of the wing, very moist.

Wing tip: tip part of the wing

Wing: The wing of the bird located next to the breast and ribs, composed of drumette, flat and wing tip.

Wishbone: This is a forked bone found in birds, it is located in between the neck and the breast.
