Chicken tenderloin: attached to the underside of the breast near the breastbone, is the innermost muscle of the breast. Arguably the most tender part alongside the oyster muscles.

Drumette, the drum of the wing: The first part of the wing attached to the breast. It is the meatiest part of the wing and resembles the chicken drumstick, though smaller.

Heel (of Chef knife): The rear part of the blade, near the handle. Often used for chopping bones or tough ingredients.

Knuckle: (poultry) end of the leg bone that connects to the foot.

Seam: the connection where one muscle ends and another begins. Seam cutting is a style of butchering, where the butcher separates individual muscles by following where they connect with the knife.

Subcutaneous fat: Fat that lies under the skin

Subcutaneous: under the skin

Supreme: chicken breast removed from the ribcage and keel, the wing drumette is still attached to the breast. The skin can be left on or removed

Wing Flat: The middle part of the wing, very moist.
