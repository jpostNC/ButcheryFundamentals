Breast: part of all poultry or birds, the pectoral muscles, tender and contains little connective tissue, the tenderloin is connected to the breast by a seam

Butcher's twine: string used to tie roasts or steaks

Leg: (poultry) the lower part of a bird, contains the thigh and drumstick. Can be purchased with part of the backbone attached. Located at the bottom of the chicken leg. Contains elastin and thicker connective tissue.

Oyster: (In poultry) - a muscle located on either side of the backbone just above where the thigh bone connects to the backbone. Oyster shaped muscles. Very tender and flavourful.

Thigh: (poultry) The meatiest part of the leg, it is full of collagen, and carries more flavour than the breast. It is located on the top part of the leg and is attached to the backbone.

Trussing : A technique of tying the bird with kitchen twine so the wings and the legs remain close to the body. When roasting poultry this technique ensures a more even cooking as well as a compact well - formed final product.

Wing: The wing of the bird located next to the breast and ribs, composed of drumette, flat and wing tip.
