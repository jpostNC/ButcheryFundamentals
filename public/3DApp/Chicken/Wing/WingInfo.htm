Chicken lollipop: a Frenched wing flat where the meat is cut on one end, and pushed down, one bone is removed, one bone stays. It creates a lollipop appearance.

Breast: part of all poultry or birds, the pectoral muscles, tender and contains little connective tissue, the tenderloin is connected to the breast by a seam

Cross contamination: It is the physical movement of transferring bacteria from one surface to another.

Frenching: removing all connective tissue from bone ends. This is done to improve the aesthetic of the bone, to make it look more elegant.

Heel (of Chef knife): The rear part of the blade, near the handle. Often used for chopping bones or tough ingredients.

Salmonella: One of the most common types of food poisoning caused by bacteria. Salmonella lives in the intestinal tract of animals. It is transmitted by eating food contaminated with animal feces

Sanitizer: a chemical solution used to make surfaces of hands, shoes, equipment, tools, and tables, clean from harmful bacteria

Stock: flavourful liquid made by extracting flavour from bones and vegetables by simmering in water.

Wing: The wing of the bird located next to the breast and ribs, composed of drumette, flat and wing tip.

Wingtip: The end part of the wing, very little meat.
