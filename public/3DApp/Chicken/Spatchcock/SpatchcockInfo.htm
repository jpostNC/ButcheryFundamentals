Breast: part of all poultry or birds, the pectoral muscles, tender and contains little connective tissue, the tenderloin is connected to the breast by a seam

Keel bone: (poultry) breastbone that runs along the middle of the chicken's cavity. An excellent source for making chicken stock.

Neck: (poultry) extension of the spinal cord into the head, very bony, very flavourful

Shears: (kitchen shears) heavy scissors used in butchery

Spatchcock: Also known as butterflying, spatchcocking is when a butcher, chef, or pro home cook (like you) removes a chicken's backbone so that it lays flat. It's a skill every poultry lover should know and master.
