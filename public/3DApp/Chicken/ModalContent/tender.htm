The tenderness of a piece of poultry is related to the type of muscle, the amount of connective tissue, and age of the bird. The older a bird gets the more connective tissue develops and the tougher the meat becomes.
Connective tissue also increases with use or exercise of the muscle
The older an animal gets and the more a muscle is used the more flavour it develops.
The tenderest cuts tend to be the least flavourful.
On poultry- the white meat- the breast, tenderloin and the drumette are the most tender.
The dark meat- The leg and wing are tougher but have more flavour.
