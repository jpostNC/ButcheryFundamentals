The majority of chickens raised in Canada for their meat are:

Plymouth White Rock or Cornish White Rock

Other birds commonly consumed: Turkey, duck, goose, guinea, pigeon (squab)

Game birds: quail, partridges, pheasant, wild duck

Chickens are not native to North America. They were brought to North America by the European settlers.
Pigeon (Squab) are also not native to north america.
Turkeys, ducks and geese are native to North America.
Turkeys were hunted and trapped by indigeous peoples of Ontario. They were also domesticated by some cultures. First nations people roasted, stuffed, cured, smoked and stewed turkeys. (Kuhnlein, Humphries)
In his book Cooking with the Wolfman, Chef David Wolfman offers up several turkey recipes including Apple Cider Brined and Roasted Turkey with Cranberry Sauce.
