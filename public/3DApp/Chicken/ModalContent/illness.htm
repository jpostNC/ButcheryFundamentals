Salmonella is a type of bacteria that can cause food borne illness, they live in the digestive tract of humans and animal and are passed by feces.
Salmonella infections are a food borne illness that are caused by undercooked meat, or unsafe food handling practices.
Salmonella is commonly associated with poultry.

Symptoms usually occur between 6 to 72 hours after you ingest the bacteria & severity of symptoms depend on how many bacteria you ingest.

The most common symptoms include:
Diarrhea, which may contain blood or mucous
Fever, Headache, stomach cramps
Nausea, vomiting, dehydration (especially among infants and the elderly)

Good practices to avoid contamination:
be aware of cleaning and sanitizing anything that touches raw poultry such as: your hands, knives, cutting boards, dishes, countertops, the package the poultry was purchased in.
Never use a contaminated knife tool surface to prepare another piece of food unless it has been cleaned or sanitized.
Cook poultry to the recommended temperature.
