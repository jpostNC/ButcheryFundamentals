Fresh poultry is extremely perishable.
Because it has such a short shelf-life it should arrive well chilled, ideally at 32ºF (0ºC) and kept well chilled until used.
Ideally, use poultry within 24 hours of receiving.
Do not store in the fridge for more than 4 days.
Poultry often carries salmonella bacteria.
Wash and sanitize all equipment and cutting surfaces after handling poultry to avoid cross contamination with other foods.
Freezing:
Store frozen poultry at 0ºF (-18ºC) or lower until it is ready to thaw.
Thaw in its original wrapper in the refrigerator.
Allow 1 to 2 days for chickens to dethaw in the refrigerator.
Allow 2 to 4 days for larger birds to dethaw in the refrigerator.
If pressed for time, thaw in cold, running water in original wrapper
Do not refreeze thawed poultry
