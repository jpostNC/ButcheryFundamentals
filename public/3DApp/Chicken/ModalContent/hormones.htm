Canadian chickens are NOT fed growth hormones (an illegal act since 1960)
Antibiotics are used to ensure the health & welfare of the animal and the consumer
A mandatory Animal Care Program that has a set of regulations & guidelines for the care & handling of the birds
This program emphasizes animal health cleanliness & safety throughout each step of the production cycle, with strict biosecurity measures to prevent disease, and to monitor the feed & watering management of the animals
Farmers are required to keep detailed records that are audited
This program has received the recognition by Canadian Food Inspection Agency

Biosecurity: Putting preventive measures in place to keep animals healthy has been a long-standing and successful practice on Canadian farms. Biosecurity planning helps to ensure that practices routinely carried out on your farm are beneficial to animal health. (Government of Canada, Canada Food Inspection Agency. https://inspection.canada.ca/animal-health/terrestrial-animals/biosecurity/eng/1299868055616/1320534707863https://inspection.canada.ca/animal-health/terrestrial-animals/biosecurity/eng/1299868055616/1320534707863 )

Inspection: All Poultry is subject to federal inspection which provides a guarantee of wholesomeness and that it is fit for human consumption. Inspection is required by Canadian law.

Grading is voluntary:
There are 3 grades of Poultry.
Canada A Grade (The best grade)
Canada Utility Grade
Canada C Grade
