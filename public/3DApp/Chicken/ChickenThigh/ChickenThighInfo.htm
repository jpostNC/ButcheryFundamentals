Cartilage: It is a flexible connective tissue. Its main function is to help keep joints and other structural parts in one place.

Connective tissue: Connects the skeletal muscles to the bones, it also gives structure to other tissues or organs in the body.

Deboned thigh - boneless thigh: (poultry) femur bone and

Drum or drumstick: Located at the bottom of the chicken leg. Contains elastin and thicker connective tissue.

Femur: The bone that holds the thigh meat in one place.

Joint: connections where bones meet. (In the UK “Joint of Beef” refers to a roasted piece of beef)

Leg: (bird) the lower part of a bird, contains the thigh and drumstick. Can be purchased with part of the backbone attached. Located at the bottom of the chicken leg. Contains elastin and thicker connective tissue.

Seam: the connection where one muscle ends and another begins. Seam cutting is a style of butchering, where the butcher separates individual muscles by following where they connect with the knife

Thigh: The meatiest part of the leg, it is full of collagen, and carries more flavour than the breast. It is located on the top part of the leg attached to the backbone.
