Plate: beef primal cut, section below the rib.

Poaching (fish): a method of cooking fish using gentle heat it ranges from 160 to 180°F (71 to 82ºC). Classically fish are poached in a court bouillon- an acidulated vegetable stock.

Poaching: a cooking method using gentle heat for example: 160 to 180°F (71 to 82ºC) to cook the salmon.

Pork: refers to the culinary use of pigs.

Porterhouse Steak: Cut from the rear part of the beef short loin, the porterhouse consists of a bone in a form of “T “a large piece of the tenderloin on one side and a piece of the striploin on the other.

Portion: (Verb) to cut products into smaller usually consistent sizes. “Go portion that fillet into 300g sizes.”

Prawn: Crustacean that resembles a large shrimp.

Primal Muscle (or Primal cut): When an animal is divided in primal sections. These are the primary cuts butchers make. Primal cuts are further broken down into sub-primal cuts, retail or fabricated cuts, steaks or roasts.

Prosciutto: an Italian dry-cured leg of pork.

Pulled Pork: Slow cooked and smoked pork shoulder that is then shredded into pieces.


Quahog: a large, rounded edible clam of the Atlantic coast of North America.

Quill: Translucid backbone, found inside the squid. Holds the structure of the squid. This is the feather-shaped internal structure that supports the squid's mantle and is a place for muscles to attach.


Rabbit: a plant eating mammal, with long ears & long hind legs. Native to North America. Known as the “other other white meat.”

Rack of Lamb: Comprised of seven or eight ribs and from the center of the animal. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing.

Rack of pork: roast cut from the loin with ribs attached, beef equivalent would be prime rib roast.

Rack: another word for ribs.

Ram: a male sheep that has used his reproductive system.

Reverse grip or pistol grip or dagger grip: a way of holding a knife by clenching the handle so the blade points down towards the floor, holding a blade this way is an effective way of manipulating the tip of the blade around round bones.

Rib Cage: has two sets of ribs which attach to the vertebra or backbone.

Rib Cap: (in beef) fat and muscle that sits on top of the rib.

Rib Tail: (in beef) muscle attached to the rib, resides closer to the plate.

Rib: a bone that is part of a mirrored series that connects to the spine, protects internal organs.

Ribs: A fabricated meat cut, usually associated with pork, but can also refer to beef or lamb. Is usually the most expensive cut of pork.

Roaster: The largest available size of chicken.

Round Fish: A fish classification, including species such as trout, bass, cod, pike, snapper, and salmon, which has a backbone along its upper body with a fillet located on both sides. Round fish have an eye located on each side of its head. They produce 2 fillets. Examples are trout, salmon, red snapper.

Rump: Another term for the hind leg or 4 legged animals.


Saddle: A tender cut of meat, most often rabbit, lamb, veal, or venison that is the unseparated loin from the rib to the leg, from both sides of an animal. Contains, striploin, middle and tail end of the tenderloin and part of the backbone. Classically the saddle is served boneless, stuffed with a farce, trussed, and roasted. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing, poaching.

Salmon Fillet:The flesh of a side of salmon after the bones have been removed.

Salmonella: One of the most common types of food poisoning caused by bacteria. Salmonella lives in the intestinal tract of animals. It is transmitted by eating food contaminated with animal feces.

Salted Fish or saltfish: The fish has been salted, which removes the water and preserves the fish to be shelf stable at room temperature.

Sanitize: to disinfect a surface with a chemical solution.

Sanitizer: a chemical solution used to make surfaces of hands, shoes, equipment, tools, and tables, clean from harmful bacteria.

Saran wrap: cling film, thin plastic wrap used to cover products.

Scaled: market term: the fish has been descaled.

Scaler: tool used to remove scales from fish. Scrape the scaler over the fish from tail to head to remove scales.

Scallopini: see Schnitzel

Scapula: also known as the shoulder blade, connects to the arm bone.

Schmaltz: Chicken skin that has been rendered. An alternative to butter or oil.

Schmear: (smear) an undesirable result of meat and fat being ground together where the fat melts and smears. Usually occurs if a grinder is not assembled correctly or if it gets stuck with bone fragments.

Schnitzel: Thinly pounded and tenderized cutlet. Breaded and fried. Usually cut from leg muscles. Most often used with pork, can also be used with beef and veal. German name. Scallopini in Italian.

Scimitar: A style of butcher knife used for cutting through large pieces of meat.

Scraper: tool used to scrape bone fragments off of meat.

Seam: the connection where one muscle ends and another begins. Seam cutting is a style of butchering, where the butcher separates individual muscles by following where they connect with the knife.

Shank (Beef): A locomotive muscle, the shank is often used to propel the animal forward or backwards, it is tough and full of sinew and collagen. It requires long moist heat cooking.

Shank (Beef): shank steak, from the bottom of the leg, also known as ossobuco.

Sharpening stone: Stones are used to sharpen knives, they can be made from natural stone, diamonds, or composite stones. They can be used with water or with oil to lubricate the stone. Oil should never be used on natural stones. Stones are defined by their grit measure. The higher the grit, the finer the stone. 300 grit is used to fix blades or start a new edge. 1000 grit is a common all purpose grit for beginners.

Shears: heavy scissors used in butchery.

Short loin: long loin with top sirloin removed - made up of two major muscle groups.

Shrimp: A small free-swimming crustacean with an elongated body, frequently harvested for food.

Side ribs:: extension of back ribs off the loin -length and size determined by where you cut off the belly from the loin.

Silage: fermented hay.

Silverskin removal: Silverskin is connective tissue that must be removed before cooking. If left on, it will become tough, and undesirable.

Silverskin: Is a thin membrane of connective tissue that exists on some muscles. Silverskin tissue gets tougher with age.

Simental: Breed of meat cow.

Sirloin (of Beef): a Primal Cut, comes from the French term surlonge which means under the loin.

Sirloin Cap: part of beef sirloin, also called Picanha.

Sirloin Chops: (pork) chops cut from the leg end of the loin, contains part of the hip bone and backbone.

Sirloin roast: Roast made from the sirloin.

Sirloin Tip Knuckle: Also known as a cushion, it is used for grilling, stewing, grinding. It has a chewier texture. It is also known as tip steak.

Sirloin: Large muscle from the very back of the long loin section. It is very tender and very lean. (Sanagans, 2020) Cooking methods recommended with this cut are pan -frying, grilling, pan-broil or sous vide.

Skinning: The act of removing the skin from the flesh. For fish, start at the tail of the fillet and move toward the head.

Skirt Steak: (or flank steak),(Bavette in French) Located on the inside of the abdomen wall just below the ribs, skirt steak can be either of two long, flat, well-marbled muscles: the diaphragm and the abdominal muscle. A little rough to look at, skirt steaks are versatile and full of flavor.

Slip Knot: a knot that slips along the rope or line around which it is made, also known as butcher's knot.

Smoked Fish: The fish can be either cold or hot smoked and are edible when purchased.

Sow: name of gilt (female pig prior to giving birth), after delivering her first litter

Spatchcock: Market type, refers to splitting the chicken in two by removing the neck and the backbone but leaving the two breasts connected. It is also known as a butterflied bird. It cooks evenly due to its flatter layout.

Spawn: to have offspring or to deposit eggs.

Spider steak: It's a little semi-circle weaved steak with a web of intramuscular fat that sits inside the hip on the Aitch bone, and there are only two per animal. It's small, flavourful thanks to its natural marbling.

Spring Lamb: Lamb born in the spring.

Square off: Square off

Squid Ink: Dark colour ink usually used for defense mechanisms by octopus and squid.

Steak: (fish) A cut of fish that is cut perpendicular to the spine and may include the bones.

Steak knife: angled knife.

Steaks: A piece of meat cut and portioned to a specific weight.

Steer: A desexed male cattle that will not be used for creating offspring, but for meat.

Stock: (crustacean stock), flavourful liquid made by simmering shellfish shells with vegetables, water, and other flavour enhancers. Can be used for making bisque.

String Frenching: Using butcher twine as a tool to French bones. (See Frenching)

Striploin: Can be a roast or a steak. Cut from the short loin on beef, it is a supportive muscle, so it is very tender. This is commonly known as a New York Steak.

Subcutaneous fat: Fat that lies under the skin.

Suckling pig: a young pig slaughtered between 2-6 weeks old.

Suet: is the raw, hard fat of beef, lamb or mutton found around the loins and kidney.

Support muscles: The muscles that encompass the support are the rib, loin, sirloin. These muscles like their name suggest are supportive of the locomotive muscles. They tend to be tender as they are not used for mobility.

Supreme: chicken breast removed from the ribcage and keel, the wing drumette is still attached to the breast. The skin can be left on or removed.

Sustainable: there are many definitions of sustainability, the United Nations defines it as “meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.”


T-Bone Steak: Cut from the loin, contains a part of the striploin, tenderloin, and vertebrae. Smaller portion than the Porterhouse. T-bone has a smaller piece of tenderloin. T-bone steaks are considered one of the more desirable steaks.

Tail bone: bone that continues off the spine. Tough and highly flavourful. (Known as Oxtail in beef).

Tail of tenderloin: part of the tenderloin that is closer to head of animal, it tapers off so it resembles a tail.

Tenderloin: The most tender cut on any animal. On 4 legged animals the tenderloin muscle starts at the ribs and runs into the leg. It is connected to the primal loin cut of beef and pork and the primal saddle cut of lamb.

Tendon: It is a ligament that attaches muscle to bone. It is tough and inedible.

Tentacles: (octopus) flexible limb used for grasping or moving about. Each tentacle has a set of two rows of suction cups.

Thigh: (poultry) The meatiest part of the leg, it is full of collagen, and carries more flavour than the breast. It is located on the top part of the leg and is attached to the backbone.

Tibia: is the bone between the patella (kneecap) and the trotter.

To feather off (a piece of meat): slang for cleaning off connective tissue.

To grind: The process of cutting meat into very small pieces. Sometimes called mincing.

Top sirloin heart or butt: part of top sirloin - largest of the muscles that make up top sirloin - when cut it makes what is known as heart steaks.

Top Sirloin Steak: Also Known As: Boneless Top Sirloin Steak; Top Sirloin Butt Steak, Boneless; Top Sirloin Center-Cut Steak; Top Sirloin Steak Boneless Cap Off; Top Sirloin Steak Cap Off.

Top sirloin: (Beef) Located just before the leg between the loin and the leg on the top back of the cow. Can be cut into steaks or roasts.

Tournedos: Small steak piece cut from the tail end of the beef tenderloin.

Tracing the bone: following the bone with your knife tip.

Tri tip: triangular cut of meat from the bottom sirloin.

Trim meat: protein that has been cut off of muscles or bones in the process of butchering. It is usually used for grinding, can be made into a farce or sausages or burgers.

Trotter: refers to the foot of pig.

Trout: Oily round fish found in freshwater, has pink flesh.

Trussing: Birds A technique of tying a bird with kitchen twine so the wings and the legs remain close to the body. This method ensures more even cooking as well as giving a beautiful presentation. Meat: a method used to tie meat together (whether it has had bone removed or not) into a roast so it cooks evenly and won't fall apart.

Two Tooth: In reference to sheep- as the temporary teeth fall out and are replaced by permanent ones, the sheep are called by the number of teeth they have. These are usually the first "two" incisors present & is generally an indication of age.


Univalve: type of mollusk which have a single shell (snails, cockles, abalone).


Vacuum sealer: machine used for vacuum sealing, has a lid with one heating bar and has another heating bar inside the machine. As the lid comes down the oxygen will be removed from the bag and the two heating bars will seal the bag closed. One can control settings depending on what is being packed and how much of it is being packed. Bag will be filled with air first, then the air will be removed from it.

Veal tenderloin: most tender cut of veal. See tenderloin.

Veal: refers to the meat of males calves of dairy cattle breeds.

Venison: is a term which refers to the meat of elk, deer, moose, reindeer, and caribou.

Vertebrae: the backbone or spine of the animal.


Weather: is a male sheep raised for mutton that has not used his reproductive system.

Wet Aging: Sub primal cuts are vacuumed, sealed and allowed to age without access to oxygen. Moisture loss is controlled by this process and is therefore a more economical form of aging to allow enzymes to break down connective tissue and make the muscle more tender.

White fish: refers to the colour of flesh of any number of fish, halibut is a white fish. Salmon, due to its orange-coloured flesh, is not white fish. (Whitefish is also a particular species of freshwater fish native to North America).

White meat: meat from the breasts and wings of chickens and turkeys are referred to as white meat because of its light colour. White meat has less fat, flavour, and connective tissue than dark meat.

Whole Dressed: Fish that have had their viscera, head, tail, and fins removed.

Whole: sold completely intact as caught.

Wing Flat: The middle part of the wing, very moist.

Wing steak: See bone-in striploin

Wing: The wing of the bird located next to the breast and ribs, composed of drumette, flat and wing tip.

Wings: (Squid) A pair of flap-like appendages used for moving around. Edible.

Wings Squid: A pair of flap-like appendages used for moving around.

Wingtip: The end part of the wing, very little meat.

Wishbone: This is a forked bone found in birds, located in between the neck and the breast.


Yearling: A farmer will use the term “yearling” to describe a sheep between 1 and 2 years old.
