Beef Prime Rib: a roast created from the rib primal. Also known as standing rib roast. It is a support muscle, so it is very tender with lots of fat. Great for roasting or grilling.

Beef Ribeye: Cut from the rib of beef, the bone and tail has been removed. Can be a steak or a roast.

Boneless rib steak: (in beef) rib steak still has the tail attached

Capless rib steak: beef rib steak with the cap removed.

Capless rib: beef rib with the rib cap removed

Chine: backbone

Chined: backbone removed- rib still attached

Plate: beef primal cut, it is the section below the rib

Primal Cuts or Primals: When an animal is divided in primal sections. These are the primary cuts butchers make. Primal cuts are further broken down into sub-primal cuts, and retail or fabricated cuts like steaks or roasts.

Rib Cap: (in beef) fat and muscle that sits on top of the rib

Rib Tail: (in beef) muscle attached to the rib, resides closer to the plate

Vertebrae: the backbone or spine of the animal.
