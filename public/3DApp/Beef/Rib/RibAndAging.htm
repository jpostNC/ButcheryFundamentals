Beef Aging in Canada: all beef is aged for a minimum of 10 days

Connective tissue: Connects the skeletal muscles to the bones, it also gives structure to other tissues or organs in the body.

Marbling: intramuscular fat in muscle, the high presence of marbling is used as a quality indicator in beef

Primal Cuts or Primals: When an animal is divided in primal sections. These are the primary cuts butchers make. Primal cuts are further broken down into sub-primal cuts, and retail or fabricated cuts like steaks or roasts.

Sub primal cuts: The secondary cut into a primal after it has has been removed, top round would be a sub-primal cut.

Wet Aging: Sub primal cuts are vacuumed, sealed and allowed to age without access to oxygen. Moisture loss is controlled by this process and is therefore a more economical form of aging to allow enzymes to break down connective tissue and make the muscle more tender. Iron flavours develop the longer beef ages in a bag.
