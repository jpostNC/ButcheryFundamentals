Loyalist College is located on the territory of the Huron-Wendat, the Anishnaabeg, and the Haudenosaunee people. We acknowledge our shared obligation to respect, honour, and sustain these lands and the natural resources contained within.

Niagara College acknowledges the land on which we gather is the traditional territory of the Haudenosaunee and Anishinaabe peoples, many of whom continue to live and work here today. Introduction

Welcome to the beef module. In this module you will meet Mike and Lieschen Beretta of Beretta Farms. Butcher Dave Meli will overview the theory and techniques necessary for butchering, preparing, handling and storing beef. You will interact with beef 3D models, and complete related assessments.

Learning Objectives

After completing this module, you will be able to:

  • Identify common primal, sub-primal and restaurant cuts of beef.
  • Understand common beef butchery techniques and how to fabricate restaurant cuts from primal cuts.
  • Differentiate between tender and tough cuts of meat.
  • Identify cooking techniques to match the cut of meat.
  • Identify key issues in modern day meat processing industries.
