Beef has a delicious meaty flavour, that intensifies with ageing, the age of the animal and the cut of meat. Meat from the tenderloin has a soft, extremely tender mouthfeel. Cuts from the more active muscles can be a lot chewier but cooking them low and slow can make them fork tender. Beef can be cooked in all cooking methods.

Beef can be eaten raw as in steak tartare where it is chopped by hand or beef carpaccio where it is thinly sliced and lightly marinated, though it is not recommended by food safety experts that this is done. It is important to know where your food comes from and how it was handled if you eat it raw. Never use store bought ground beef for tartare, since it has so many surface that have been exposed to oxygen and potential harmful bacteria.

A good way of remembering the tenderness of animals is that the more the muscle works, the tougher the meat becomes, so meat from the front back and lower regions is tough. The meat in the middle section; the rib, loin and the sirloin are all very tender because they mainly just support and are not active.
