Veal is the by-product of the dairy industry. For dairy farmers that sell milk for a living, calves that are born male have traditionally been seen as a burden. Veal are young male dairy calves that have been fed milk or fresh pasture and are slaughtered around 6 months.

Veal meat is prized for being tender and sweet. The colour of the meat is a paler form of red than traditional beef, but when raised properly should be darker than pork.
