Certified Black Angus- is a brand of beef in the USA and Canada that must meet 10 quality indicators in the Prime and AAA grades to meet the Certified Black angus quality standard.

Kobe- is a type of Japanese Beef.

Wagyu- means “Japanese beef”. The breed of Tajima-Gyu cattle produce Kobe beef and it must be raised in the Hyogyo Prefecture. This beef is very distinct from Canadian beef because it has way more marbling, and is therefore highly prized, and expensive. Kobe A5 is the highest grade possible. Only beef raised and graded in Japan can be labelled as “Kobe”

Wagyu- In Canada is a cross breed of Tajima and Black Angus, bred outside of Japan. It is often mis-labelled as Kobe beef due to its high marbling.

Grass Fed Beef- Cattle that are raised and finished on grass, this produces different flavour in the animal than grain fed beef. It is earthier, grassier, and is not as tender as grain fed beef.
