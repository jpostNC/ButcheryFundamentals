Beef Tenderloin: Most tender cut of beef, located under the loin in the belly cavity, it starts at the ribs and runs into the leg. It is further cut into: “Filet Mignon”- center cut steaks; “Chateau Briand”, the head of the tender loin which sits in the leg; and the tail portion of the tenderloin which are called ‘Tournedos”

Chain: a long thin muscle attached to the side of a tenderloin, very tender.

Silverskin: Is a thin membrane of connective tissue that exists on some muscles. Silverskin tissue gets tougher with age.

Tenderloin: The most tender cut on any animal. On 4 legged animals the tenderloin muscle starts at the ribs and runs into the leg. It is connected to the primal loin cut of beef and pork and the primal cut saddle in lamb.
