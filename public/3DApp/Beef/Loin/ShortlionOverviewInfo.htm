Backbone: the vertebrae or spine of an animal

Beef Tenderloin: Most tender cut of beef, located under the loin in the belly cavity, it starts at the ribs and runs into the leg. It is further cut into: “Filet Mignon”- center cut steaks; “Chateau Briand”, the head of the tender loin which sits in the leg; and the tail portion of the tenderloin which are called ‘Tournedos”

Bone-in striploin or wing steak: Striploin steak with part of the vertebrae still attached.

Long loin: short loin with top sirloin

Porterhouse Steak: Cut from the rear part of the beef short loin, the porterhouse consists of a bone in a form of “T “a large piece of the tenderloin on one side and a piece of the striploin on the other.

Short loin: long loin with top sirloin removed - made up of two major muscle groups

Striploin: Can be a roast or a steak. Cut from the short loin on beef, it is a supportive muscle, so is very tender. This is commonly known as a New York Steak.

T-bones steaks: Cut from the front part of the short loin consists of a bone in a form of “T “a small piece of the tenderloin on one side and a piece of the striploin on the other.

Wing steak: See bone-in striploin
