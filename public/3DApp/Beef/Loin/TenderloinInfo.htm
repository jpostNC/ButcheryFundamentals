Chain: a long thin muscle attached to the side of a tenderloin, very tender.

Connective tissue: Connects the skeletal muscles to the bones, it also gives structure to other tissues or organs in the body.

Elastin: connective tissue that does not break down or dissolve when cooked.

Filet Mignon: Steak cut from the center part of the beef tenderloin

Porterhouse Steak: Cut from the rear part of the beef short loin, the porterhouse consists of a bone in a form of “T “a large piece of the tenderloin on one side and a piece of the striploin on the other.

Silverskin: Is a thin membrane of connective tissue that exists on some muscles. Silverskin tissue gets tougher with age.

Tournedo: Small steak piece cut from the tail end of the beef tenderloin.

Vertebrae: the backbone or spine of the animal.
