Anatomy: the branch of science concerned with bodily structure of humans, animals, and other living organisms, especially as revealed by dissection and the separation of parts. (Oxford Dictionary, 2021)

Beef Rib: Primal cut, fabricated cuts are ribeye, prime rib. Very tender, second most expensive cut of beef.

Bleed out animals: after slaughter animals are hung and bled to remove all blood from their veins. If the blood remains it could taint and rot the meat.

Chateau Briand: The head of the beef tenderloin.

Eviscerated: refers to an animal or fish that has had their internal organs removed. It is important that this process is done as soon as possible after slaughter to prevent the organs from spoiling the muscles. Removing the organs also assists in cooling the carcass. Cooling is imperative to get the carcass out of the danger zone before harmful bacteria can breed.

Grading process: a grader will cut between the 12 and 13th rib while carcass is hanging - inspectors use a card to grade accordingly to quantity of fat cap and the amount of intramuscular marbling. Also looks at the colour of fat and colour of muscle to determine grading. Grading comes in AAA, AA, A, B or Prime Grading

Marbling: intramuscular fat in muscle, the high presence of marbling is used as a quality indicator in beef

Sirloin (of Beef): a Primal Cut, comes from the French term surlonge which means under the loin.

Tenderloin: 2/3 of it is in the short loin, 1/3 including chateaubriand is in sirloin

Tendon: It is a ligament that attaches muscle to bone. It is tough and inedible

Beef Short loin: a primal cut, contains part of the tenderloin, and the striploin. Fabricated cuts are the porterhouse and T-Bone.

Vertebrae: the backbone or spine of the animal.
