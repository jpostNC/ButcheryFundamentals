Eye of round: muscle that comes from the leg, it is lean but tough. Cooking methods recommended with this cut are grilling, roasting, braising

Femur: The femur & humerus bones are large bones from the leg they are full of marrow and can be split to be served as roasted marrow bones. They add lots of flavour to stocks.

Flank: Primal cut, surrounds abdomen. Fabricated cut: flank steak

Forcemeat: ground protein (meat or fish) emulsified with fat (a burger is an example of forcemeat)

Gooseneck: outside round and eye of round connected before being taken apart.

Heel meat: three muscles from edge of shank - rat's tail, merlot steak and spider steak

Inside Round: A large muscle from the inside of the leg. Medium tender cut, can be grilled or pan roasted, skewered or stewed. Also known as “top round.”

Merlot steak: Similar to flank steak, it has long muscle fibers. Located at the hind shank below the bottom. Its nature is tender. Cooking methods recommended with this cut grilling, pan- frying, sous vide

Ossobuco: Italian dish made from the beef shank, often braised at a low temperature.

Outside Round: A large muscle from the outside of the leg. Medium tender cut, can be grilled or pan roasted, skewered, or stewed. Also known as bottom round.

Picanha: Portuguese name for steak off on the top sirloin, it has a triangular shape.

Primal Cuts or Primals: When an animal is divided in primal sections. These are the primary cuts butchers make. Primal cuts are further broken down into sub-primal cuts, and retail or fabricated cuts like steaks or roasts.

Shank (Beef): As part of the locomotive muscles, the shank is often used to propel the animal forward or backwards, it is tough and full of sinew and collagen. It requires long cooking. Cooking methods recommended with this cut are braising, pressure cooking, sous- vide.

Sirloin tip: or sirloin tip knuckle

Spider steak: a small steak that sits in the aitch bone. Not generally marketed because of its size and that there are only 2 on each animal.

Tenderloin butt: chateaubriand

Top sirloin: Located just before the leg. You can cut steaks or roast.

Trim: meat that is collected in the process of cleaning meat and bones. Usually ground up and used for burgers or sausages or forcemeat.
