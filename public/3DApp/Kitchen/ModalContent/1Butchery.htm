Butchery is the act of processing animals into smaller cuts. Traditionally a butcher would slaughter, eviscerate, butcher and sell the meat from animals. In Ontario animals are slaughtered in federally and provincially inspected facilities. The carcasses are then eviscerated and aged for a period of time before either being sold whole, split or cut into primal cuts. Butchers today also prepare value added products like meat pies, sausages, bacon, terrines and salami.

Primal Muscle (or Primal cut): When an animal is divided in primal sections. These are the primary cuts butchers make. Primal cuts are further broken down into retail or fabricated cuts, steaks or roasts.

Fabricated Cut: a piece of meat butchered into an edible cut; a steak or a tied roast or a chicken thigh are examples of a fabricated cut. A whole rib is a primal cut.

Butcher: is a person who processes animals into primal cuts, sub primal cuts, fabricated cuts, roasts and steaks and sells them. Classically a butcher would also slaughter and eviscerate animals.

Meat cutter: a person who cuts meat into smaller cuts, usually they would butcher primal cuts into fabricated cuts.

Charcuterie: French term for prepared, cured and preserved meats, sausages, pates, salami.

Charcutier: a person whose job is to make charcuterie.
