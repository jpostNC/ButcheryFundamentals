Butcher's hook: used as an extension of the hand to hook and hold meat, can be used to protect the hand and/or prevent meat from slipping.

Butcher's twine: string used to tie roasts or steaks

Needle (Larding): a tool used to lard meat.

Larding: to insert fat into meat

Mesh Glove: a metal glove used to protect the non-knife hand.

Chain mail glove: See mesh glove

Global: brand of Japanese knife

Fish tweezers: tweezers used to remove pin bones from fish

Scaler: tool used to remove scales from fish

Bone saw: (Butchers saw, Hacksaw) : Saw used for cutting bone. Not used for cutting meat.

Aitch bone: (Aitchbone, Aitch-bone) is the back section of the pelvic bone which is connected to the hip bone

Needle (Larding): a tool used to lard meat.

Larding: inserting fat into usually tough cuts of meat to add moisture and flavour.

Band saw: electric saw, with a band-style blade, used for cutting bones and steaks in a butcher shop. Meat is usually pre cut to the bone before going on the band saw.

Meat packing plants: large companies that slaughter and process animals into primal, subprimal and fabricated cuts.

Filleting knife: same shape as a boning knife but can be longer and is very flexible which allows blades to glide through flesh and over fish bones.

Scaler: tool used to remove scales from fish. Scrape the scaler over the fish from tail to head to remove scales.

Oyster knife or shucker: A knife used to open the shell of oysters.
