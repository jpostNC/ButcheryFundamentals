French Knife: A style of chef's knife with a slightly curved blade that can range from 6-20 inches long. It is the go to knife for most cooks and can be used for cutting almost everything. The term French knife is sometimes used interchangeably as “Chef's knife”

Boning Knife: a butcher's knife with a blade that ranges from 6-12 inches, it has a curved tip and can be sturdy or flexible. It is used for removing meat from bone. It is not used to cut bone. Secretion labeled Knives and tools breakdown

Honing Steel: a tool used to keep an edge on western knives. A Honing steel will not sharpen a knife but rather keeps the edge true. Japanese knives are only sharpened on stones, honing steels are not used. They can be made with steel, porcelain or diamond.

Sanitizer: a chemical solution used to make surfaces of hands, shoes, equipment, tools, and tables, clean from harmful bacteria

Sanitize: to disinfect a surface with a chemical solution.

Left handed station set up: organize knives on the left side of the cutting board with blade facing to the left away from the board.
