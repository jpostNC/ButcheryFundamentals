Scimitar: A style of butcher knife used for cutting through large pieces of meat.

Honesuki: a Japanese boning knife- it usually has a 80/20 beveled edge

Cleaver: A large heavy rectangular shaped knife used for chopping through bone.

Mallet: resembles a hammer, used to tenderize meat. One side has large dimples and one has smaller dimples, one side can also be flat.

Reverse grip or pistol grip or dagger grip: a way of holding a knife by clenching the handle so the blade points down towards the floor, holding a blade this way is an effective way of manipulating the tip of the blade around round bones.

Bevel: angle of an edge.

Chine bone: Back bone which is attached to the rib section of the animal.

Band saw: electric saw, with a band-style blade, used for cutting bones and steaks in a butcher shop. Meat is usually pre cut to the bone before going on the band saw.

Schnitzel: Thinly pounded and tenderized cutlet. Breaded and fried. Usually cut from leg muscles. Most often used with pork, can also be used with beef and veal.
