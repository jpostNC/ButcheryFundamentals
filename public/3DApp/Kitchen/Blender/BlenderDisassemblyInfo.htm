Instructions for Meat Grinder Disassembly

  1. Unplug the machine
  2. Remove the meat pusher and food pan from the meat chamber.
  3. Unscrew the ring retainer.
  4. Pull out the auger slightly.
  5. Remove the plate and the blade
  6. Remove the auger.
  7. Unscrew the chamber head.
  8. Wash, sanitize and dry all parts. Note a bamboo skewer is a great tool for pushing meat out of the grinder plate

Chamber head: L shaped tubular part of meat grinder that holds the auger.

Auger: Part of a meat grinder that resembles a drill bit, it pushes meat towards the grinder blade and plate.

Grinder Blade: a + (cross) shaped metal cutter with 4 sides, one of each of the 4 sides is sharpened into a blade. The sharpened side presses up against the grinder plate. When the auger spins it pushes the meat against the grinder plate and the grinder blade cuts it and forces it through the plate and the end result is ground meat.

Food pan: top part of meat grinder that holds the meat before it is ground.

Meat Pusher: Tool used to push meat into the meat grinder, resembles a cylinder.

Grinder plate: a circular metal plate with holes used in a meat grinder- the size of the holes determines the size of the ground meat that will be pushed through the plate.

Retaining ring: Holds the grinder plate and grinder blade in place on the chamber head.

Body of grinder: the engine that operates the parts of a meat grinder.

Schmear: (smear) an undesirable result of meat and fat being ground together where the fat melts and smears. Usually occurs if a grinder is not assembled correctly or if it gets stuck with bone fragments.
