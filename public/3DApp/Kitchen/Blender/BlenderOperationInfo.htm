Meat grinder parts: have been removed from freezer and should be cold prior to usage

Forcemeat: ground protein (meat or fish) emulsified with fat (a burger is an example of forcemeat) desired ratio is about 20%fat to 80% meat.

Emulsification: (in butchery) blending fat and meat together.

Schmear: (smear) an undesirable result of meat and fat being ground together where the fat melts and smears. Usually occurs if a grinder is not assembled correctly or if it gets stuck with bone fragments.

Grinder plate: a metal plate with holes used in a meat grinder- the size of the holes determines the size of the ground meat that will be pushed through the plate.

Double grinding application: double grind meat to emulsify meat and fat even further into forcemeat

Grinder Assembly

  1. Make sure grinder parts are cold
  2. Ensure meat has been dried and is kept over ice while assembling grinder
  3. Have an additional empty bowl over ice to catch meat once the grinding process starts
  4. Place meat on top of food tray
  5. Using the plastic meat pusher ( to push down the meat into chamber leading to grinder blade
  6. Do not try to force too much meat at once into chamber
