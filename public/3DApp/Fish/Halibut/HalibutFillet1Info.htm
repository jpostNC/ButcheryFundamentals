Fish Stock: flavourful liquid made by simmering fish bones with vegetables and other flavour enhancers.

Oily Fish: Oily fish have oil in their flesh tissue, belly cavity and guts. Their fillets can contain up to 30% oil. Oily fish are a very good source of vitamin A and D and are also very high in Omega 3 fatty acids which are an anti-inflammatory for arthritis and have health benefits for cardiovascular disease. They have two fillets per fish. Can be found in the ocean or freshwater. Types of oily fish include Sardines, Tuna, Salmon and Trout.

White fish: refers to the colour of flesh of any number of fish, halibut is a white fish. Salmon, due to its orange-coloured flesh, is not white fish. (Whitefish is also a particular species of freshwater fish native to North America, but not used this module)
