Aquaculture: The farming of fish and or other marine plants or animals.

Farm Raised Fish: indicates fish that has been raised in one of many styles of aquaculture. Not wild caught.

Fillet: (Noun) A boneless piece of fish, usually a whole side.

Fillet: (Verb): To remove the bones from a piece of fish.

Fin: Exterior part of fish, assist in swimming and steering.

Gill: Organs that allow fish to breathe underwater.

Round Fish: A fish classification, including species such as trout, bass, cod, pike, snapper, and salmon, which has a backbone along its upper body with a fillet located on both sides. Round fish have an eye located on each side of its head. They produce 2 fillets. Examples are trout, salmon, red snapper

Trout: Oily round fish found in freshwater, has pink flesh.

To determine freshness fish should smell like the ocean, they should not smell “fishy” or of ammonia.
Their flesh should be pliable when touched.
Their eyes should be clear.
Their gills should be red.
Their scales should not fall off easily.
