The government of Canada refers to Mollusks and Crustaceans as Shellfish, but they are 2 distinct categories of marine life.

Fish, crustaceans and mollusks are sometimes collectively referred to as seafood.

Crustaceans and mollusks are sometimes collectively referred to as shellfish.

Crustaceans

“Crustaceans are aquatic animals that have jointed legs, a hard shell and no backbone, such as crab, crayfish, lobster, prawns and shrimp. Most mollusks have a hinged two-part shell and include clams, mussels, oysters and scallops, as well as various types of octopus, snail and squid.”
Government of Canada, Crustaceans and Mollusks, Priority Food Allergens, https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/food-nutrition/reports-publications/food-safety/seafood-fish-crustaceans-shellfish-priority-food-allergens.html

Shellfish- Mollusks

Mollusks: are soft sea animals and invertebrates. They make up approximately 23% of all marine life.

Mollusks have a mantle (a dorsal body wall that covers their visceral mass) and a nervous system.

Mollusks can be subclassified into three major groups:

Univalves: which have a single shell (snails, cockles, abalone)

Bivalves: which have a pair of hinged shells (scallops, oysters, mussels, clams)

Cephalopods: have a bilateral body system, a prominent head and arms or tentacles (examples: octopus, cuttlefish)
