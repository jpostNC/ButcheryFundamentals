Abductor muscle (shellfish): Muscle designed to open and close the shells, allowing water and food particles in.

Bivalves: type of mollusk which have a pair of hinged shells (scallops, oysters, mussels, clams are examples).

Oyster knife or shucker: A knife used to open the shell of oysters

Oyster Shells: have one flat top and one bowl shaped shell, keep the bowl-shaped shell down on ice or salt so it holds the oyster retains the natural oyster liquor.
