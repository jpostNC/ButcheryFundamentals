Abductor muscle (shellfish): Muscle designed to open and close the shells, allowing water and food particles in, found in oysters clams and mussels.

Bivalves: type of mollusk which have a pair of hinged shells (scallops, oysters, mussels, clams are examples).

Cherrystone: Hard shell clam, comes from the east coast of North America. A bivalve mollusk.

Clams: type of bivalve mollusk.

Littleneck: The smallest of the clams

Quahog: a large, rounded edible clam of the Atlantic coast of North America.
