Anadromous: Fish born in freshwater that spend most of their life in the sea and then return to freshwater to spawn. Example: Salmon, smelt, striped bass and sturgeon.

Atlantic Salmon: A species of salmon that is farmed. Wild Atlantic salmon are listed as an endangered species.

Pin Bones: (on Fish) long thin bones that resemble pins. Generally, in round fish the pin bones run from a third of the way down the body toward the head and sideways from the backbone.

Rib Cage: has two sets of ribs which attach to the vertebra or backbone.

Round Fish: A fish classification, including species such as trout, bass, cod, pike, snapper, and salmon, which has a backbone along its upper body with a fillet located on both sides. Round fish have an eye located on each side of its head. They produce 2 fillets. Examples are trout, salmon, red snapper.

Spawn: to have offspring or to deposit eggs.

Whole Dressed: Fish that have had their viscera, head, tail, and fins removed.
