Whole carcasses of lamb can be held at 0-2℃ with good air circulation for upwards of 10 days, depending on the amount of fat cover.

State of the art processing plants can guarantee shelf life of chilled lamb cuts in their packaging for up to 90days if held between 0 – 2℃.

Freshly ground lamb can be held at 0-2 ℃ (32-35 ℉) for 48 hours.

Roasts and steaks can be held at 0-2 ℃ (32-35 ℉) for 5 - 7 days.

The best practice for all proteins is to vacuum seal them. This takes up the least amount of space and can prevent cross contamination. Vacuum sealing also limits the exposure to moisture and air, which will delay bacterial growth.
