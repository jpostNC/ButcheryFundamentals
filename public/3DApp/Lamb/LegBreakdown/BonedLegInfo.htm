Collagen: Collagen fibers form bundles of meat fibers together. Younger animal collagens have fewer crosslinks which make them easier to cook. Tender cuts have a relatively thinner mesh of collagens thus allowing them to be tender while tougher cuts will have a more numerous collagen fiber therefore making them more resistant to heat. Collagen is also responsible for whether cooked meat ends up tough or tender.

Connective tissue: Connects the skeletal muscles to the bones, it also gives structure to other tissues or organs in the body.

Lamb Hind shank: shank cut from the back leg. Best for braising or grinding.

Lamb Leg: Consists of 5 main muscles the Inside Round, Outside Round, Eye of the round, Knuckle, Sirloin as well as the shank.
