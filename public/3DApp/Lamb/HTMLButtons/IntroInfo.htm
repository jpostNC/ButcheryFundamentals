Backbone: the vertebrae or spine of an animal

Lamb Foreshank: shank that connects to the fore shoulder.

Lamb Hind shank: shank cut from the back leg. Best for braising or grinding.

Lamb Leg: Consists of 5 main muscles the Inside Round, Outside Round, Eye of the round, Knuckle, Sirloin as well as the shank.

Lamb Neck: The neck is a long line of bones that is sweet and tender when cooked slowly. Best for stewing. Also known in the UK as “the best end”.

Lamb Shoulder: The shoulder is one of the most used muscles of the animal, therefore making it less tender. This cut is used most often for stews and braises. Shoulder muscles may also be boned out - connective tissue removed and prepared for roasting low and slow.

Lamb: a sheep under one year of age when slaughtered. Typically, in the lamb's life cycle, they are born in the spring, this can be manipulated by farmers, to produce lambs being born year-round.

Rack of Lamb: Comprised of seven or eight ribs and from the center of the animal. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing.

Saddle: A tender cut of meat, most often lamb, veal or venison that is the unseparated loin from the rib to the leg, from both sides of an animal. Contains, striploin, middle and tail end of the tenderloin and part of the backbone.

Tenderloin: The most tender cut on any animal. On 4 legged animals the tenderloin muscle starts at the ribs and runs into the leg. It is connected to the primal loin cut of beef and pork and the primal saddle cut of lamb.
