Belly/ Breast: This extends from the ribs and it is fatty, therefore its best for grilling or braising. (Breast: Lamb primal cut.) (Belly: Pork Primal Cut)

Elastin: connective tissue that does not break down or dissolve when cooked.

Lamb Loin: Primal cut also known as the saddle, contains loin, and parts of the tenderloin and vertebrate.

Saddle: A tender cut of meat, most often lamb, veal or venison that is the unseparated loin from the rib to the leg, from both sides of an animal. Contains, striploin, middle and tail end of the tenderloin and part of the backbone. Classically the saddle is served boneless, stuffed with a farce, trussed, and roasted. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing, poaching.

Seam: the connection where one muscle ends and another begins. Seam cutting is a style of butchering, where the butcher separates individual muscles by following where they connect with the knife.

Silverskin removal: Silverskin is sinew that must be removed before cooking. If left on, it will become tough, and undesirable.

Silverskin: Is a thin layer of connective tissue that exists on some muscles. Connective tissue that gets tougher with age.
