Boneless shoulder (in Lamb): the deboned shoulder, usually tied for roasting

Connective tissue: Connects the skeletal muscles to the bones, it also gives structure to other tissues or organs in the body.

Gland: parts of the animal's anatomy that are responsible for many functions, they can be found within muscles. They are edible but will spoil faster than muscles.

Lamb Foreshank: shank that connects to the fore shoulder.

Lamb Shoulder: The shoulder is one of the most used muscles of the animal, therefore making it less tender. This cut is used most often for stews and braises. Shoulder muscles may also be boned out - connective tissue removed and prepared for roasting low and slow.

Scapula: also known as the shoulder blade, connects to the arm bone.

Silverskin: Is a thin membrane of connective tissue that exists on some muscles. Silverskin tissue gets tougher with age.
