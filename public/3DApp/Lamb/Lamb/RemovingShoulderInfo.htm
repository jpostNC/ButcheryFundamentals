Connective tissue: Connects the skeletal muscles to the bones, it also gives structure to other tissues or organs in the body.

Lamb Foreshank: shank that connects to the fore shoulder.

Lamb Shoulder: This is the most used muscle of the animal, therefore making it less tender. This cut is used most often for stews and braises. Shoulder muscles may also be boned out - connective tissue removed and prepared for roasting low and slow.

Scapula: also known as the shoulder blade, connects to the arm bone.

Seam: the connection where one muscle ends and another begins. Seam cutting is a style of butchering, where the butcher separates individual muscles by following where they connect with the knife.
