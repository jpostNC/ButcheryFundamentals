Deer: Deer (Cervidae) is a family of antlered, hoofed ruminants native to North America. There are 5 deer species in Canada: Caribou, Elk, Moose, Mule Deer, White Tail Deer. (Assinewe, Valerie, Deer of Canada, Nature Canada, 05/24/2015/ https://naturecanada.ca/news/blog/deers-of-canada/)

Eyes of the loin: larger is the loin end - the one with a flap is the shoulder end

Silverskin: Is a thin membrane of connective tissue that exists on some muscles. Silverskin tissue gets tougher with age.

Venison: is a term which refers to the meat of elk, deer, moose, reindeer, and caribou.
