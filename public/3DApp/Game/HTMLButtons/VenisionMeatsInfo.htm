Bison: a humpback, shaggy wild ox that is native to North America. Sometimes referred to as Buffalo- though it is not. Bison is butchered and fabricated in the same way as beef, though its hump has different muscles than beef, and is considered a delicacy.

Deer Rack: Refers to the rack of ribs but can also mean antlers.

Elk: Member of the deer family, larger than deer, native to north America.

Free Range: Chickens or animals that have access to the outdoors and pasture.

Game: is a term applied to wild animals that we eat. They are categorized as large and small. Elk, deer, moose, reindeer, caribou are classified as large game and are collectively known as Venison. Buffalo, wild boar, and bear are other large animals that live in the wild. Small game animals are rabbits, squirrels, beavers, and muskrats. The taste of wild game varies from animal to animal and throughout the year because, in nature, animals eat different foods at different times of the year.

Grass Fed: Animals whose diet consists of grass and has not been supplemented with grain.

Loin: cut of meat that runs on either side of the spine, very tender, support muscle.

Marbling: intramuscular fat in muscle, the high presence of marbling is used as a quality indicator in beef

Porterhouse Steak: Cut from the rear part of the beef short loin, the porterhouse consists of a bone in a form of “T “a large piece of the tenderloin on one side and a piece of the striploin on the other.

T-Bone Steak: Cut from the loin, contains a part of the striploin, tenderloin, and vertebrae. Smaller portion than the Porterhouse. T-bone has a smaller piece of tenderloin. T-bone steaks are considered one of the more desirable steaks.

Tenderloin: The most tender cut on any animal. On 4 legged animals the tenderloin muscle starts at the ribs and runs into the leg. It is connected to the primal loin cut of beef and pork and the primal saddle cut of lamb.

Top Sirloin Steak: Also Known As: Boneless Top Sirloin Steak; Top Sirloin Butt Steak, Boneless; Top Sirloin Center-Cut Steak; Top Sirloin Steak Boneless Cap Off; Top Sirloin Steak Cap Off

Venison: is a term which refers to the meat of elk, deer, moose, reindeer, and caribou.
