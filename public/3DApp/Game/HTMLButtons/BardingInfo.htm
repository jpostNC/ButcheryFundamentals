Barding: to cover a piece of meat with fat to add moisture and flavour.

Caul fat: thin see-though fat membrane that surrounds the internal organs of animals. Pork caul fat is the most common, it is used to wrap roasts and steaks to add moisture, also used to wrap items together.

Butcher's twine: string used to tie roasts or steaks.

Deer: Deer (Cervidae) is a family of antlered, hoofed ruminants native to North America. There are 5 deer species in Canada: Caribou, Elk, Moose, Mule Deer, White Tail Deer. (Assinewe, Valerie, Deer of Canada, Nature Canada, 05/24/2015/ https://naturecanada.ca/news/blog/deers-of-canada/ )

En Crepinette: wrapped in caul fat.

Farce: forcemeat, made by mixing ground protein with other flavours and ingredients to make a stuffing.

Loin: cut of meat that runs on either side of the spine, very tender, support muscle.

Saddle: A tender cut of meat, most often rabbit, lamb, veal or venison that is the unseparated loin from the rib to the leg, from both sides of an animal. Contains, striploin, middle and tail end of the tenderloin and part of the backbone. Classically the saddle is served boneless, stuffed with a farce, trussed and roasted. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing, poaching.

Trussing: a method used to tie meat together (whether it has had bone removed or not) into a roast so it cooks evenly and won't fall apart.
