Backbone: the vertebrae or spine of an animal or fish

Loin: cut of meat that runs on either side of the spine, very tender, support muscle.

Rib: a bone that is part of a mirrored series that connects to the spine, protects internal organs.

Saddle: A tender cut of meat, most often lamb, rabbit, veal or venison that is the unseparated loin from the rib to the leg, from both sides of an animal. Contains, striploin, middle and tail end of the tenderloin and part of the backbone. Classically the saddle is served boneless, stuffed with a farce, trussed and roasted. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing, poaching.
