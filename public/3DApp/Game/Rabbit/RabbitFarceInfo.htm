Backbone: the vertebrae or spine of an animal or fish

Caul fat: thin see-though fat membrane that surrounds the internal organs of animals. Pork caul fat is the most common, it is used to wrap roasts and steaks to add moisture, also used to wrap items together.

Farce: forcemeat, made by mixing ground protein with other flavours and ingredients to make a stuffing.

Loin: cut of meat that runs on either side of the spine, very tender, support muscle.

Offal: Also known as variety meats, these are the animal innards or extremities that can be used in cooking or in production of foods such as sausage. The following are examples: Heart, liver, kidneys, lungs.

Kidneys: The kidneys are two reddish-brown bean-shaped organs found in vertebrates.

Pancetta: Italian cured pork belly

Saddle: A tender cut of meat, most often lamb, rabbit, veal or venison that is the unseparated loin from the rib to the leg, from both sides of an animal. Contains, striploin, middle and tail end of the tenderloin and part of the backbone. Classically the saddle is served boneless, stuffed with a farce, trussed and roasted. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing, poaching.

Tenderloin: The most tender cut on any animal. On 4 legged animals the tenderloin muscle starts at the ribs and runs into the leg. It is connected to the primal loin cut of beef and pork and the primal saddle cut of lamb.

Trussing: Birds A technique of tying a bird with kitchen twine so the wings and the legs remain close to the body. This method ensures more even cooking as well as giving a beautiful presentation.
