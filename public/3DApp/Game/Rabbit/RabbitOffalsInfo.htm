Cavity: space in an animal where the stomach and innards once were.

Farce: forcemeat, made by mixing ground protein with other flavours and ingredients to make a stuffing.

Offal: Also known as variety meats, these are the animal innards or extremities that can be used in cooking or in production of foods such as sausage. The following are examples: Heart, liver, kidneys, lungs.

Kidneys: The kidneys are two reddish-brown bean-shaped organs found in vertebrates.

Rabbit: a plant eating mammal, with long ears & long hind legs. Native to North America. Known as the “other other white meat.”

Saddle: A tender cut of meat, most often lamb, veal or venison that is the unseparated loin from the rib to the leg, from both sides of an animal. Contains, striploin, middle and tail end of the tenderloin and part of the backbone. Classically the saddle is served boneless, stuffed with a farce, trussed and roasted. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing, poaching.
