Aitch bone: (Aitchbone, Aitch-bone) is the back section of the pelvic bone which is connected to the hip bone

Hare: A larger animal than a rabbit, they look similar but are different species. Hare meat is red and the flavour is a lot gamier than rabbit.

Hind legs: legs from the rear of a four-legged animal, sometimes called the rump.

Loin: cut of meat that runs on either side of the spine, very tender, support muscle.

Offal: Also known as variety meats, these are the animal innards or extremities that can be used in cooking or in production of foods such as sausage. The following are examples: Heart, liver, kidneys, lungs.

Kidneys: The kidneys are two reddish-brown bean-shaped organs found in vertebrates.

Rabbit: a plant eating mammal, with long ears & long hind legs. Native to North America. Known as the “other other white meat.”

Rack: another word for ribs

Saddle: A tender cut of meat, most often rabbit, lamb, veal or venison that is the unseparated loin from the rib to the leg, from both sides of an animal. Contains, striploin, middle and tail end of the tenderloin and part of the backbone. Classically the saddle is served boneless, stuffed with a farce, trussed and roasted. Very tender cut great for grilling, roasting, pan searing, poaching.

Trim meat: protein that has been cut off of muscles or bones in the process of butchering. It is usually used for grinding, can be made into a farce or sausages or burgers.
