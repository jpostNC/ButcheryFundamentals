In Newfoundland, restaurants that have a license can serve moose on their menu. This moose is hunted in its natural habitat and an important part of animal control in the Province. Additionally, partridge and rabbit can also be served along with forged berries (such as partridgeberries or bakeapples (cloudberries), a Newfoundlandf favorite) . Hunting moose is a way of life on the island and serving it in restaurants helps preserve the culture

In Canada, wild game such as moose meat is not Federally inspected. The only expected compliance is to have this meat stored separately from Domestic meat. In Ontario, game such as bison, boar, elk and caribou served by restaurants today is farmed. However in Newfoundland, they are allowed to hunt, kill and serve moose and caribou that are hunted in their natural habitat.
